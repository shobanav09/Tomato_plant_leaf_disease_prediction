Pathogens


Leaf mold is caused by the fungus Passalora fulva. It is not known to be pathogenic on any plant other than tomato.

Symptoms & Signs


  • Disease symptoms first appear after seven days as light green spots on leaves.
  • Soon after, olive-green asexual conidia emerge, and eventually the spots become necrotic.
  • Disease symptoms appear first on the lower leaves of tomato plants.
  • Fruit production may not be severely hampered during the earlier stages of disease, but over time, disease severity increases and defoliation occurs.
  • Proper identification of disease is important.
  • Abiotic conditions such as magnesium deficiency and the beginning stages of other diseases can produce similar symptoms to tomato leaf mold.
